Problem #7: WE ARE WIRED!!!

Background Information: A connection strip is made up of a series of posts; half of them are white, the other half black. Each of the black posts must be connected by a wire to one white post and each white post must be connected to one black post. For simplicity, we will consider the length of wire used for a single connection to be the number of posts the white is from the black. For example, here is a possible connection strip (with the posts numbered):


        ○       ○       ●       ○       ●       ●       ○       ● 
        
        1       2       3       4       5       6       7       8

Programming Problem:

INPUT- 	    From a file named input7, a sequence of non-negative integers, one value 
            per line. Each non-zero integer will represent a run of the same colored
            posts. The sequence of integers will end with a 0. You should assume that
            the first post is white. The input below corresponds to the graphic above. 
OUTPUT-	    To a file output7, the length of wire needed to connect the posts and 
            a list of the connections, one connection per line.
